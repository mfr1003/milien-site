Michigan Law

Know Your
Deadlines

The clock starts the moment work is done. These dates are not flexible.

20

Notice of Furnishing

Subcontractors and suppliers must serve this within 20 days of first furnishing labor or materials. Miss it and your lien protection is weakened.

90

Claim of Lien

All lien claimants have 90 days from last furnishing to record a Claim of Lien with the county Register of Deeds. This is a hard deadline.

0

Demand Letter

Not legally required, but we always recommend sending one first. Resolves 40-60% of disputes without ever touching a lien form. No deadline, but send it today.

105

BDVR-35 Filing Deadline

Application to the Michigan Department of State must be filed within 105 days of the last day work was performed on the vehicle. This is the lien attachment date.

30

Certified Letter After TR-42

Once MDOS issues the TR-42 Certificate, you have 30 days to send certified letters to all owners, lienholders, and MDOS with itemized charges and sale details.

75

Earliest Vehicle Sale

The public sale of the vehicle cannot occur until at least 75 days after the TR-42 issuance date. The entire process cannot exceed 225 days from lien attachment.

Questions

Straight
Answers

Is this the same as hiring a lawyer?
No. LodgeMiLien is a document preparation service, not a law firm. We prepare the documents required under your state's property lien statutes. If you have a complex dispute or need to enforce a lien in court, we'll point you to an attorney. Most situations never get there.
What if I already missed the 20-day Notice window?
You may still have partial lien rights. The Claim of Lien deadline is separate. Tell us your dates during intake and we'll calculate exactly where you stand.
Does this work for commercial jobs too?
Yes. Property lien statutes in all states we serve cover both residential and commercial projects. The documents and process are similar, though commercial projects may have additional considerations we'll flag during review.
I'm a supplier, not a contractor. Does this apply to me?
Yes. Material suppliers who furnish to a job site have lien rights under Michigan law, subject to the same 20-day Notice of Furnishing and 90-day Claim of Lien deadlines.
What counties in Michigan do you cover?
All 83 Michigan counties. The Claim of Lien must be filed in the county where the property sits, and we include county-specific filing instructions with every package.
